### **Anticipated Reviewer Concerns**

1. **Label Noise & Proxy Validity**: Reviewers will challenge whether arXiv absence from a conference truly indicates rejection. Papers may be: (a) never submitted, (b) under review elsewhere, (c) withdrawn by authors, or (d) posted *after* acceptance elsewhere. This creates false positives in the rejection label.

2. **Selection Bias**: The decision to post on arXiv is non-random. Authors of borderline papers, junior researchers, or certain subfields may be more likely to use arXiv as a "safety net," confounding the rejection signal with author/seniority effects.

3. **Temporal Mismatch**: arXiv posting dates may not align with conference submission cycles. A paper posted *after* a conference deadline could be intended for the next year's submission, not a rejection from the current year.
