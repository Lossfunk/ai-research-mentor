I hear you—breaking into AI research without built-in mentorship is tough, but absolutely doable. Here’s a concrete roadmap designed for self-driven learners, with specific programs and communities that can serve as "guided entry points."

---

### **Phase 1: Build Foundations & Find Your People (Months 0-6)**

**1. Structured Learning with Built-in Community:**
- **Fast.ai’s Practical Deep Learning**: Free, top-down approach that gets you training models week 1. Their forums are active and beginner-friendly.
- **Andrew Ng’s Machine Learning (Coursera)**: Classic foundation; join the dedicated subreddit (r/MLQuestions) or Discord study groups to avoid isolation.
- **MIT 6.036 (Intro to ML)**: Free on MIT OCW with full lectures, assignments, and exams. Self-paced but rigorous.

**2. Join Virtual Reading Groups:**
- **Papers With Code**: Start with "Trending" papers that have code. Reproduce one paper—this is your first "research project."
