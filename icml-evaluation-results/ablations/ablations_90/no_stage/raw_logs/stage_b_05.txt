This idea is not original—it's been explored extensively since at least 2017. Multiple papers have used GANs (and variants like SeqGAN, RankGAN, LeakGAN) for poetry generation and analysis.

The bigger problem: **GANs are a poor fit for poetry analysis**. GANs excel at generation, not analysis. If your goal is analysis, you're using the wrong tool entirely. For poetry analysis, you'd want discriminative models, interpretability methods, or specialized NLP approaches.

**What you should do instead:**
1. Clarify your actual goal: generation or analysis? They're different tasks requiring different architectures.
2. If generation: Look at the existing literature (Zhang et al 2017, Yi et al 2020) and identify what's *actually* missing—maybe controllability, specific poetic forms, or cross-lingual work.
3. If analysis: Use BERT-family models with attention visualization, probe classifiers, or linguistic feature analysis. GANs won't give you interpretable insights about poetic structure.

Don't waste time reinventing a 7-year-old wheel. Find a specific, unsolved problem in the space first.
