This is likely a dead end unless you have a very specific angle.

SMS is brutally limiting for explainability: 160 characters, no visuals, no interactivity. Most XAI methods rely on feature importance charts, counterfactual examples, or confidence scores—all nearly impossible to convey clearly via text. For low-literacy users, it's even harder.

**The core problem:** If a farmer gets advice like "Plant maize next week," explainability means answering "Why?" and "What if I don't?" Doing this via SMS without patronizing or confusing them is an unsolved problem.

**Before you go further:**
1. **What's the AI actually doing?** (Pest ID? Market prediction? Fertilizer advice?) The explainability needs differ massively.
2. **How is this different from existing agri-SMS services?** Companies like Digital Green and Esoko have been doing SMS-based agri advice for years. What does "explainable" add?
3. **Have you tested comprehension?** Most "explainable" systems make sense to engineers but fail with actual users. Have you done user testing with farmers?

**My take:** The innovation isn't in the AI—it's in the *communication design*. If you can figure out how to make explanations simple, actionable, and trustworthy within SMS constraints, that's valuable. But the AI part is probably secondary.

Search for "SMS agricultural advice systems" and "explainable AI low-literacy users" first. I suspect you'll find the explainability gap is well-documented and unsolved for good reason.
