## My Recommendation

**Start with AI ethics** if you're:
- More interested in big-picture questions (bias, fairness, accountability)
- Coming from humanities/social sciences background
- Want to contribute meaningfully within weeks
- Prefer reading/discussion over building things

**Start with robotics safety** if you're:
- Already comfortable with programming and physics
- Prefer concrete, measurable problems
- Excited by hands-on engineering challenges
- Patient with a steeper 6-12 month learning curve

## The Compromise: "Safe AI Systems"

You don't have to choose completely. There's a hybrid area focusing on **technical AI safety** (alignment, robustness, verification) that combines the philosophical rigor of ethics with concrete engineering. It's more technical than AI ethics but doesn't require hardware.
