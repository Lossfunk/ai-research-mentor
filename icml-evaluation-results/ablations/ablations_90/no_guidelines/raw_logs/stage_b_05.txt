Using GANs for poetry **analysis** (as opposed to generation) could be original depending on your specific approach, but you need to navigate a crowded literature carefully. Here's a breakdown:

### **Not Original: GANs for Poetry Generation**
If you mean generating poetry, that's well-trodden ground. Researchers have applied GANs to text generation for years (SeqGAN, TextGAN, etc.), and poetry generation specifically has multiple existing studies. This would be a hard sell for novelty.

### **Potentially Original: GANs as an *Analytical Tool***
This is where it gets interesting. Using the GAN *framework* to study poetry's properties is far less explored. Consider these angles:

1. **Discriminator as a "Poetic Quality" Model**: Train a discriminator to distinguish between human-written and machine-generated poetry (or between different poetic forms/eras). Analyze its learned features to understand what it "thinks" defines poetic quality, meter, or style. This is more interpretability research.

2. **Adversarial Perturbation Analysis**: Use the generator to create minimal perturbations of poems that fool the discriminator. This could reveal stylistic boundaries and sensitive features (e.g., "changing which words breaks the sonnet structure?").

3. **Latent Space Exploration**: Train a GAN to generate poems conditioned on specific features (emotion, meter, era). Interpolate in latent space to study how poetic elements blend and transitionâ€”this becomes a computational lens on poetic structure.

4. **Counterfactual Generation for Stylistic Analysis**: Generate counterfactuals ("How would Dickinson rewrite this Shakespearean sonnet?") to study stylistic fingerprints quantitatively.

### **Critical Challenges & Alternatives**
- **Text GANs are unstable**: The discrete nature of text makes GANs difficult. Many have moved to diffusion models or large-scale autoregressive transformers (GPT-style) for generation.
- **Evaluation crisis**: How do you validate that your "analysis" reveals real insights, not just artifacts? You'll need collaboration with literary scholars and rigorous human evaluation.
- **Consider these instead**: 
  - **VAEs** offer more interpretable latent spaces for analysis
  - **Probing classifiers** on frozen LLMs might be more robust for feature analysis
  - **Style transfer frameworks** could be repurposed for analytical insights
