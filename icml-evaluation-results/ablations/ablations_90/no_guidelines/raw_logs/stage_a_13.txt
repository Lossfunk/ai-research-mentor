### **1. Tap Into Publicly Available Health Datasets**
Many high-quality datasets are freely accessible:
- **PhysioNet**: 100+ clinical datasets (ECG, EEG, ICU waveforms)
- **NIH repositories**: 
  - Cancer Imaging Archive (radiology scans)
  - Sequence Read Archive (genomic data)
  - All of Us Research Program (opens summer 2024)
- **Kaggle**: 200+ health competitions with data (e.g., RSNA pneumonia detection, COVID-19 forecasting)
- **Google Health Data**: COVID-19 search trends, mobility reports
- **WHO Global Health Observatory**: Disease burden, risk factors
- **MIMIC-III/MIMIC-IV**: ICU data (requires free training certification)
- **UK Biobank**: Large-scale biomedical database (free for researchers)

### **2. Generate Synthetic Health Data**
When real data is restricted, create realistic alternatives:
- **Synthea**: Open-source tool that generates synthetic patient lifespans with realistic medical histories
- **MedGAN/TableGAN**: Generate synthetic EHR data that preserves statistical properties
- **CT-GAN**: Create synthetic tabular health data
- **DICOM simulators**: Generate synthetic medical images with tools like `pydicom`

### **3. Partner Strategically**
Your public health knowledge makes you an ideal collaborator:
- **University hospitals**: Offer to help with IRB proposals or domain expertise for data access
- **Public health departments**: Many have unused data but lack AI capacity
- **Global health NGOs**: Partners in Health, PATH, Doctors Without Borders increasingly use AI
- **Research consortia**: Join groups like OHDSI (Observational Health Data Sciences) with common data models

### **4. Contribute Without Direct Data Access**
- **Algorithm development**: Create noise-robust models, federated learning frameworks, or privacy-preserving techniques
- **Systematic reviews**: Use AI to analyze publication patterns, identify research gaps, or automate evidence synthesis
- **Benchmarking**: Reproduce published models on public subsets and identify limitations
- **Simulation modeling**: Build agent-based models of disease transmission using public parameters

### **5. Join Open Challenges**
- **Grand Challenges**: Gates Foundation, NIH, and others fund health AI competitions
- **Codalab**: Machine learning competitions often provide data
- **DrivenData**: Social impact challenges with health datasets

### **6. Focus on Methodological Innovation**
- **Federated learning**: Develop methods for training on decentralized data
- **Transfer learning**: Adapt ImageNet/NLP models to health with minimal data
- **Uncertainty quantification**: Critical for clinical deployment
- **Bias detection**: Audit existing health AI models for fairness
