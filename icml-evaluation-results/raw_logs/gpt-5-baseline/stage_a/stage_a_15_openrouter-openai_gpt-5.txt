You can break in from anywhere by joining remote-first communities, contributing tiny artifacts, and using structured programs that pair beginners with mentors. Here’s a focused playbook with concrete places to go, what to ship first, and how to ask for help.

Where to find remote projects and mentorship (low barrier)
- Open research communities
  - ML Collective (MLC): open Slack, reading groups, office hours, “research jams.”
  - fast.ai forums: project-focused study groups; very welcoming to beginners.
  - Hugging Face forums/Discord: help channels, eval/data/model repos, frequent “good first issue.”
  - EleutherAI and LAION Discords: eval, data, interpretability, LLM infra (beginner channels exist).
  - OpenMined: privacy-preserving ML (DP, federated); courses plus project channels.
  - Safety/ethics: AI Safety Camp (team projects), Alignment Jams, ML4H community for health.
  - Robotics (sim-first): ROS Discourse, Webots and PyBullet Discords, Stable-Baselines3.

- Structured, remote programs (apply when open; prep meanwhile)
  - ML Reproducibility Challenge (ICLR/NeurIPS seasons): reproduce a paper with support.
  - Google Summer of Code and Outreachy: many AI/ML orgs (scikit-learn, OpenMined, NumFOCUS projects).
  - AI Safety Camp (team-based research sprints).
  - KaggleX or similar fellowships/internships (varies by season).

- Remote-friendly contribution targets (research-adjacent)
  - Evaluation/safety: lm-evaluation-harness, OpenAI Evals, HELM, Guardrails libs.
  - NLP/LLMs: Hugging Face Transformers/Datasets/Evaluate, sentence-transformers.
  - Interpretability: TransformerLens, Captum.
  - RL/Robotics: Stable-Baselines3, Safety-Gymnasium/safe-control-gym.
  - Vision/geo: TorchVision, TorchGeo.
Tip: filter GitHub issues by “good first issue,” “help wanted,” “documentation,” or “examples.”

Your first 4 weeks (2–5 hours/week)
- Week 1: Pick a lane and say hello
  - Choose one area: eval/safety, interpretability, vision, RL, or health.
  - Join 2 communities from the list; post a short intro: who you are, your goal, and a tiny thing you’ll ship in 2 weeks.
  - Set up a public GitHub “research-log” repo with a notes.md and a results.csv.

- Week 2: Run a baseline and log it
  - Reproduce one tiny result end-to-end (e.g., CIFAR-10 5–10 epochs; evaluate an LLM on 100 prompts; run PPO on CartPole).
  - Save results to CSV, pin versions/seeds, and make one plot. Share a link in the community channel and ask for 1 suggestion.

- Week 3: Claim a beginner-friendly task
  - Find 1 “good first issue” (docs, example, small bug, or eval config).
  - Post your 3–5 bullet plan in the project channel before coding; ask, “Does this approach look right?”
  - Open a PR within the week (even if small). Respond to feedback quickly.

- Week 4: Draft a micro-project proposal and recruit 1–2 collaborators
  - One page: question, dataset/sim, metrics, 3 steps, risks, and Definition of Done.
  - Share it in a community channel; invite 1–2 people to pair on a 2–3 week sprint.
  - Schedule a 30‑minute kickoff; agree on scope, seeds, and deliverables.

Remote-collab hygiene (helps mentors say yes)
- Be async-first: use issues/PRs for decisions; summarize calls in writing.
- Reproducible by default: environment file, dataset links, fixed seeds, commands in README.
- Small commits; open PRs early; add before/after screenshots or plots.
- Time zones: propose 2–3 slots in UTC; rotate meeting times if needed.
- Definition of Done for each task: one script/notebook that runs, a results row, and a 3–5 bullet summary.

How to ask for help (templates)
- Discord intro (5 lines)
  - Hi! I’m [Name], [location/time zone], interested in [area]. This week I’ll [tiny goal]. Here’s my repo/notebook: [link]. I’d value feedback on [one specific question].
- “Can you sanity-check my plan?” message
  - Context: 1–2 lines on the task and why it matters.
  - Plan: 3 bullets (data, method, metric).
  - Ask: “Does this evaluation look fair? Anything I’m missing?”
- Cold email/DM (10–15 min ask)
  - Subject: Quick feedback on a 1‑page plan for [topic]
  - Body: who you are (1 line), tiny artifact link, 1‑page plan link, and a precise question. Thank them and offer to follow up with results.

Low-compute project starters you can do remotely
- Eval-only: 50–100 prompt mini-benchmark for LLM safety or hallucinations; compare 2–3 open models; release dataset + script.
- Reproducibility: replicate a small paper’s main figure; report deviations and seeds; open a docs/example PR.
- Interpretability: replicate a known circuit with TransformerLens; add one ablation; contribute a tutorial.
- RL/Robotics (sim): add a safety wrapper (action limits/emergency stop) to a Gym task; plot reward vs violations.
- Health (public/synthetic): run DP-SGD on MedMNIST; report utility–privacy trade-off; write a short Model/Data Card.

Finding opportunities and staying accountable
- Watch channels for “project pitches,” “office hours,” and “mentorship” threads; come with a link to your artifact.
- Apply to seasonal programs 2–4 weeks before deadlines with a tiny working demo; reviewers favor concrete progress.
- Form a “mentors by committee”: 2–3 maintainers/researchers who give micro-feedback a few times per month.
- Keep a weekly public update: what you did, a plot/screenshot, next week’s goal, and one blocker.

Resources and support (remote-friendly)
- Compute: Colab/Kaggle (free GPUs), Hugging Face Spaces/community hardware grants (limited), request dataset mirrors from maintainers if bandwidth is an issue.
- Learning: fast.ai Practical DL, Hugging Face Course, MIT 6.S191 (YouTube) with captions.
- Inclusivity/affinity groups (often with mentorship): WiML, Black in AI, LatinX in AI, Queer in AI.
