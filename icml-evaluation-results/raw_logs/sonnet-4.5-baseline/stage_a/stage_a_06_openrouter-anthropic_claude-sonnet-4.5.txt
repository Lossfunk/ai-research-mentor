## üí° **The "Math Workaround" Strategy:**

### **What you WILL need (unavoidable):**
- **Basic statistics**: Mean, standard deviation, p-values, confidence intervals
  - *Level*: AP Statistics or intro college course
  - *Why*: To evaluate experiments
  
- **Conceptual understanding**: What neural networks do (not the calculus behind them)
  - *Level*: Watch 3Blue1Brown videos
  - *Why*: To read papers and understand results

### **What you CAN avoid:**
- ‚ùå Backpropagation derivations
- ‚ùå Optimization proofs
- ‚ùå Probability theory proofs
- ‚ùå Linear algebra beyond basic concepts
- ‚ùå Calculus beyond understanding "gradient = slope"

### **How to survive paper reading:**
- Skip the "Methods" math sections
- Focus on: Introduction, Experiments, Results, Discussion
- Use tools: ChatGPT to explain equations in plain English
- Look for "applied" papers, not "theoretical" ones
