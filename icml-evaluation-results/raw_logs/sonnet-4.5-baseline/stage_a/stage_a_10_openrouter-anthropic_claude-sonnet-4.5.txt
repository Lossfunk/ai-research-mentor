## üî¨ **Finding Research Mentors at Your University**

### **Step-by-Step Cold Email Strategy:**

**Step 1: Identify potential mentors (Week 1)**
- Search your university's CS/AI faculty
- Look for:
  - ‚úÖ Assistant professors (often more accessible)
  - ‚úÖ Research interests matching yours
  - ‚úÖ Recent publications (active research)
  - ‚úÖ Mentions of "welcoming undergrads"

**Step 2: Do your homework (Week 2)**
- Read their recent papers (at least abstracts)
- Check their lab website
- See if they have undergrad researchers already
- Note specific projects that interest you

**Step 3: Craft your email (Week 3)**

**EMAIL TEMPLATE:**

```
Subject: First-Gen Student Interested in [Specific Research Area]

Dear Professor [Name],

I'm [Your Name], a [year] [major] student at [University]. As a first-generation college student, I'm working to find pathways into AI research and your work on [specific topic] particularly resonates with me.

I recently read your paper "[Title]" and was fascinated by [specific detail - shows you actually read it]. I'm especially interested in [connection to your interests/background].

My background includes:
- [Relevant coursework]
- [Any projects, even small ones]
- [Skills: Python, etc.]

I understand you may not have openings, but I'd be grateful for:
1. 15 minutes to discuss potential research opportunities
2. Advice on building research skills as a first-gen student
3. Suggestions for other faculty I might reach out to

I'm happy to work around your schedule. Thank you for considering my request.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Contact info]
[LinkedIn if you have it]
```

**Step 4: Send to 10-15 professors**
- Expect 30-50% response rate
- Don't be discouraged by silence
- Follow up once after 1 week if no response

**Step 5: The meeting**
- Come prepared with questions
- Ask about their path into research
- Express genuine interest
- Ask: "What would you recommend for a first-gen student like me?"

## üìö **Building Research Skills Without a Mentor (For Now)**

### **Self-Guided Research Apprenticeship:**

**Month 1: Learn to Read Papers**
- **Resource**: "How to Read a Paper" (S. Keshav) - Google it, free PDF
- **Practice**: Read 1 paper/week from arXiv
- **Tool**: Connected Papers (visualize paper relationships)
- **Community**: Post summaries on Twitter/LinkedIn (get feedback!)

**Month 2: Replicate a Simple Paper**
- **Pick**: Recent paper with code available (Papers With Code)
- **Goal**: Run their code, understand results
- **Document**: Write blog post explaining it
- **Share**: Post on Medium, dev.to (free platforms)

**Month 3: Extend the Work**
- **Modify**: Try different dataset or parameter
- **Analyze**: What changed? Why?
- **Write**: Document findings
- **Portfolio**: Add to GitHub

**Result**: You now have research experience to show mentors!
