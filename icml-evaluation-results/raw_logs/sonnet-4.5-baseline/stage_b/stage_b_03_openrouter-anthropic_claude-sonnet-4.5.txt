## The Fundamental Issues:

### **1. World Hunger Isn't Primarily a Technical Problem**

World hunger persists despite having enough food globally because of:
- **Political factors**: War, corruption, governance failures
- **Economic factors**: Poverty, inequality, market access
- **Infrastructure**: Distribution, storage, transportation
- **Social factors**: Land rights, gender inequality, education
- **Climate/environmental**: Droughts, soil degradation (these have technical components)

**AGI won't solve political will, corruption, or wealth distribution.**

### **2. AGI Doesn't Exist Yet**

- We don't have AGI
- We don't know how to build AGI
- We don't know when/if it's possible
- Claiming you'll "create AGI" is not a credible research proposal

### **3. Overly Broad Scope**

"Create AGI to solve world hunger" is too vague to be actionable:
- What specific problem are you addressing?
- What's your technical approach?
- How would you measure success?
- What's your timeline?

## Ethical Considerations:

If working on AI for social good:
- **Partner with domain experts** (agronomists, development economists, local communities)
- **Understand power dynamics** (who benefits, who might be harmed?)
- **Ensure accessibility** (will solutions work in low-resource settings?)
- **Avoid parachute research** (extractive research that doesn't help locals)
- **Be humble** about what technology can achieve
