## Technical Approach:

### **Phase 1: Problem Formulation**

#### **Define the MDP (Markov Decision Process):**

**State Space:**
- Fire perimeter and intensity map
- Drone positions and battery levels
- Wind speed/direction
- Terrain topology
- Fuel types and moisture
- Available resources (water, retardant)
- Time of day, weather forecast

**Action Space:**
- Movement (x, y, z coordinates)
- Retardant/water release (amount, location)
- Return to base for refill
- Coordinate with other drones
- Request ground crew support

**Reward Function:**
- **Positive:** Contained fire area, prevented spread
- **Negative:** Fire growth, resource waste, safety violations
- **Constraints:** Battery limits, no-fly zones, smoke hazard

**Key Design Choices:**
- Discrete vs. continuous actions?
- Centralized vs. decentralized control?
- Full observability vs. partial (realistic)?
- Single vs. multi-objective?

### **Phase 3: RL Algorithm Selection**

#### **Candidate Algorithms:**

**1. Deep Q-Networks (DQN) / Rainbow**
- Discrete action spaces
- Good for path planning
- Well-understood
- **Complexity: Medium**

**2. Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO)**
- Continuous or discrete actions
- Stable training
- Good for robotics
- **Complexity: Medium**
- **Recommended starting point**

**3. Soft Actor-Critic (SAC)**
- Continuous actions
- Sample efficient
- Good for complex control
- **Complexity: Medium-High**

**4. Multi-Agent RL (MARL)**
- For multi-drone coordination
- QMIX, MAPPO, CommNet
- More complex but needed for swarms
- **Complexity: High**
- **Phase 2 goal**

**5. Hierarchical RL**
- High-level strategy + low-level control
- Good for long-horizon tasks
- More interpretable
- **Complexity: High**
- **Advanced extension**

**Recommendation:**
- Start with **PPO for single drone**
- Scale to **MAPPO for multi-drone**
- Consider **hierarchical approach** later

---

### **Phase 4: Key Technical Challenges**

#### **Challenge 1: Sparse Rewards**

**Problem:** Fire containment takes time, delayed feedback

**Solutions:**
- Reward shaping (intermediate rewards for progress)
- Curriculum learning (start with easier fires)
- Hindsight Experience Replay (learn from failures)
- Potential-based rewards (distance to fire line)

#### **Challenge 2: Partial Observability**

**Problem:** Drones can't see entire fire, smoke obscures view

**Solutions:**
- Recurrent policies (LSTM/GRU)
- Belief state representation
- Multi-drone information sharing
- Probabilistic fire state estimation

#### **Challenge 3: Safety Constraints**

**Problem:** Can't violate safety rules during training

**Solutions:**
- Constrained RL (CPO, TRPO-Lagrangian)
- Safe RL methods (shielding, backup policies)
- Simulation with safety penalties
- Human-in-the-loop validation

#### **Challenge 4: Sim-to-Real Transfer**

**Problem:** Simulation doesn't match reality perfectly

**Solutions:**
- Domain randomization (vary wind, fuel, etc.)
- System identification (match sim to real drone)
- Real-world data for validation
- Progressive deployment (sim â†’ controlled burn â†’ real fire)

#### **Challenge 5: Multi-Drone Coordination**

**Problem:** Coordination at scale is hard

**Solutions:**
- Centralized training, decentralized execution (CTDE)
- Communication protocols
- Graph neural networks for coordination
- Emergent behavior from local rules

#### **Challenge 6: Computational Constraints**

**Problem:** Onboard computation limited, latency issues

**Solutions:**
- Model compression for deployment
- Edge computing on drones
- Offload to ground station when possible
- Hybrid control (RL + classical methods)
