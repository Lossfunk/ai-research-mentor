That said, here's a **general roadmap** that works for most beginners:

## Immediate Next Steps

**1. Build foundational skills**
- **Math**: Linear algebra, calculus, probability/statistics
- **Programming**: Python (NumPy, pandas, PyTorch or TensorFlow)
- **ML basics**: Take a structured course like:
  - Andrew Ng's Machine Learning or Deep Learning Specialization (Coursera)
  - Fast.ai's Practical Deep Learning for Coders
  - Stanford CS229 or CS231n (freely available online)

**2. Learn by doing**
- Work through tutorials and replicate classic papers
- Participate in Kaggle competitions or similar challenges
- Contribute to open-source ML projects

**3. Read actively**
- Follow recent papers on arXiv (start with survey papers in your area of interest)
- Read blogs from top labs (OpenAI, DeepMind, Google Research, Meta AI)
- Join communities (r/MachineLearning, Twitter/X AI research community, Discord servers)

**4. Find a research direction**
- Identify problems that interest you
- Look for gaps in existing work
- Connect with researchers or join a lab if possible
