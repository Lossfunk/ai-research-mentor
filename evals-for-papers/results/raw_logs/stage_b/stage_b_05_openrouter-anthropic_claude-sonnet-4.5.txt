## Potential Novel Angles (If You Insist on GANs)

### A. **Adversarial Style Analysis**
- Train a discriminator to distinguish poetry styles
- Analyze learned features to understand stylistic differences
- **Test**: Does the discriminator capture known stylistic markers? Does it discover new ones?

### B. **Counterfactual Poetry Generation for Analysis**
- Use GANs to generate "counterfactual" poems (e.g., "What would this poem look like in another style?")
- Analyze differences to understand style-specific features
- **Test**: Do generated counterfactuals preserve semantic content while changing style?

### C. **Adversarial Robustness for Poetry Classifiers**
- Use adversarial examples to probe weaknesses in poetry analysis models
- Improve classifier robustness through adversarial training
- **Test**: Does adversarial training improve generalization to unseen poets/styles?

### D. **GAN-Based Data Augmentation**
- Generate synthetic poetry to augment small datasets for analysis tasks
- **Test**: Does GAN-augmented training improve downstream classification accuracy?

## More Promising Alternatives (Non-GAN)

If your goal is actually **poetry analysis**, consider:

### 1. **Transformer-Based Approaches**
- Fine-tune BERT/RoBERTa for poetry classification
- Use attention weights to interpret what the model focuses on
- **Why better**: State-of-the-art for text understanding

### 2. **Computational Poetics**
- Combine NLP with literary theory
- Analyze meter, rhyme, alliteration, imagery using rule-based + ML
- **Why better**: Interpretable, grounded in domain knowledge

### 3. **Contrastive Learning**
- Learn representations by contrasting different poets/styles
- **Why better**: Effective for learning style embeddings without GANs

### 4. **Explainable AI for Poetry**
- Apply SHAP, LIME, or attention visualization to poetry classifiers
- **Why better**: Directly addresses "analysis" goal

## Tests You Should Run (If Pursuing GANs)

### 1. **Baseline Comparisons**
- Compare against non-GAN methods (transformers, CNNs, RNNs) on the same analysis task
- Metrics: Accuracy, F1, interpretability of learned features

### 2. **Qualitative Analysis**
- What does the discriminator learn? Visualize learned features
- Generate synthetic examples—do they capture poetic qualities?

### 3. **Ablation Studies**
- Is adversarial training necessary, or does a standard classifier work as well?
- What happens with different GAN architectures (WGAN, StyleGAN-style)?

### 4. **Domain Expert Evaluation**
- Have literary scholars evaluate generated/analyzed poetry
- Do computational findings align with literary theory?

### 5. **Generalization Tests**
- Train on one corpus (e.g., Romantic poetry), test on another (e.g., Modernist)
- Does the model capture universal vs. era-specific features?
