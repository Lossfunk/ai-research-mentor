## Intuition
The phrase "GANs for poetry analysis" could mean many things: using GANs to generate poetry and then analyze it, using adversarial training to improve poetry classifiers, or something else entirely. ## Why this is principled
Originality hinges on the **task definition** and **technical contribution**. The search reveals that transformers (GPT-2, LSTMs) have largely replaced GANs for poetry generation [P1][P5][P6]. GANs are notoriously hard to train on discrete text and have fallen out of favor for NLP. If you're proposing GANs for an *analysis* task (classification, retrieval, style transfer evaluation), you'd need to show why adversarial training beats standard supervised or self-supervised methods. One recent analysis paper [P8] uses human-labeled emotion datasets with standard classifiers, not GANs.
